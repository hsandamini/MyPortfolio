I'm H.L.D. Hiruni Sandamini, a final-year undergraduate reading for a BSc (Hons) in Statistics at the Department of Statistics, Faculty of Applied Sciences, University of Sri Jayewardenepura, Sri Lanka.

My academic path began with a competitive selection into the Mathematics, Statistics and Economics stream, where I spent my first two years building a broad quantitative foundation — from differential equations and linear algebra to microeconomics, macroeconomics, and probability theory. Over the course of my degree I've developed particular strength in experimental design, time series analysis, distribution theory, and statistical inference, alongside practical exposure to R programming, regression analysis, medical statistics, actuarial statistics, and stochastic processes. I'm currently looking to apply this training in research, data analysis, or industry roles where rigorous quantitative reasoning makes a real difference.
